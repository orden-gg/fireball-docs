Services are background automations that handle repetitive tasks for you. Unlike dungeon runs and fishing sessions that you start manually, services run continuously on a schedule — checking your account and taking action when needed.

## Available Services

Fireball offers four automated services:

| Service            | What It Does                                                       | Configurable                        | Free with Scroll of Fire |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------ | ----------------------------------- | ------------------------ |
| **Fish Seller**    | Automatically sells your fish for seaweed                          | Yes — preserve counts per rarity    | Yes                      |
| **ROM Claimer**    | Claims ROM resources (shards, dust, GIGUS dust) when near capacity | Yes — energy conversion + threshold | Yes                      |
| **Loot Collector** | Breaks pots and chests to collect loot items                       | No                                  | Yes                      |
| **Hatchery**       | Maintains egg hatching conditions (temperature, comfort, fate)     | Yes — per-egg thresholds            | Yes                      |

## How Services Work

1. **Subscribe** to a service from the Services page
2. **Configure options** (if available) for that service
3. The service runs automatically in the background
4. **Reveal results** anytime to see what's been accumulated since your last check

Services require an active Gigaverse session and consume Sparks per operation.

<Callout type="info">
  The **Scroll of Fire** grants free access to all services, bypassing Spark costs entirely. Without
  it, each service invocation costs Sparks.
</Callout>

## Fish Seller

Automatically sells your fish for seaweed. Runs approximately 20 checks per hour.

### Configuration: Untouchable Supply

You can set a **preserve count per rarity** — the Fish Seller will keep that many fish of each rarity and only sell the rest. Set a preserve count to 0 to sell all fish of that rarity, or set a higher number to keep a reserve.

## ROM Claimer

Automatically claims resources from your ROMs when they reach 90% capacity. Claimed resources include shards, dust, and GIGUS dust depending on your ROM tiers.

### Configuration

- **Convert Energy** — Toggle whether to convert excess energy to GIGUS dust
- **Energy Threshold** — Set a minimum energy reserve level (0 to your max daily energy); the claimer won't convert energy below this threshold

## Loot Collector

Automatically breaks pots and chests to collect the loot inside. Tracks recipes including Noob Chest, Juiced Chest, Blue Pot, and Tan Pot. No additional configuration needed — subscribe and it handles everything.

## Hatchery

The most configurable service. Automatically maintains your eggs in the hatchery by managing temperature, comfort, and fate levels.

### Configuration Per Egg

For each egg, you can set:

- **Temperature** (0–100) — Minimum temperature to maintain
- **Comfort** (0–5) — Comfort level to maintain (decays 1 per day)
- **Fate** (0–20) — One-time cost to reach the target fate level. Applied automatically when hatching progress reaches 90% or above
- **Faction Priorities** — Preferred faction order for hatching outcomes

You can also set **default thresholds** that apply to any egg without specific configuration.

## The Reveal Mechanic

Services accumulate results silently in the background. When you're ready to see what happened, hit the **Reveal** button to view everything since your last check.

Each service shows different reveal data:

- **ROM Claimer** — Shards by type, dust by type, GIGUS dust total
- **Fish Seller** — Fish sold by type, seaweed earned
- **Loot Collector** — Loot items collected, pots/chests broken by recipe
- **Hatchery** — Eggs hatched with progress and rarity, materials used

The reveal plays an animated overlay showing your accumulated results.

## Service History

View the full log of service operations at **Services → History**. This shows all transactions with timestamps and operation details.

## Requirements

To use services, you need:

1. An active **Gigaverse session** (check expiration in the sidebar)
2. **Sparks** for service costs (unless you have the Scroll of Fire)
3. **Giga-Juice** active on your account
